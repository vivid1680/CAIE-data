General Comments
The paper tested a wide range of AS topics. Candidates performed well on standard kinetic
and energy calculations, though unit conversions in multi-prefix quantities and specific inter-
pretations of wave graphs proved more challenging. [cite: 505, 512]
Comments on Specific Questions
Question 2
Candidates had to convert 0.25kN mm −2 toN m −2. Many failed to account for the squared
millimeter conversion (1mm 2 = 10−6 m2), which led to the selection of C instead of the correct
answer D (2.5×10 8 N m −2). [cite: 81, 505]
Question 6
This question required interpreting a velocity-time graph. Correct analysis of the area under
the graph for the first 4 seconds (12×2 + 0.5×12×2 = 36m) identified C as the correct
statement. [cite: 120, 132, 505]
Question 24
Candidates were asked for the frequency of microwaves based on melted chocolate spots at
antinodes. Recognizing that the distance between two adjacent antinodes isλ/2 (6.0cm) was
essential. Usingv=f λ, the frequency was calculated as 2.5GHz(Key C). [cite: 321, 324, 505]
